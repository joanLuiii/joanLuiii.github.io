Pet Widget

Designed an enterprise application software
for different Animal Welfare Organisations (AWOs)

Process

User interviews, Competitive analysis, Personas, Service Blue Print, Ideation, Feature Prioritization and
Usability testing

Tools

Axure RP, Sketch, Post-its, Papers, Excel Sheets, Photoshop, Illustrator

Role

UX Interaction Designer

Huge contribution in UX interaction design and interactive prototyping.
I was also actively participating in research, ideation and usability testing in the team.

The Challenge

Develop a Enterprise Application Software for animal welfare organizations (AWOs) to streamline processes and operations.

We are challenged to design this proposed platform to be straightforward, simple to use, and intuitive.

Lean UX / Agile Approach

We adopted agile and lean ux approach in a team of 3 and went through 3 rounds of Sprints in 3 weeks to come up with our MVP prototype.

Our Design Process

1. Research

Research the current problems in the pet ecosystem.
Understand who our target audience are.
Identify the strengths and
weakness of their current
process and operations.

2. Empathize

Put ourselves in the pet welfare organisations's shoes to understand what are they doing, feeling and thinking.

3. Define

Define the primary problems / pain point the pet welfare organisations is experiencing and what needs to be improved.

4. Ideate

Brainstorm creative solutions that will solve their pain points.

5. Prototype

Convert our ideation into design and build a clickable prototype.

6. Test

Do usability testing with welfare's staff & owners to gather feedback, validate our hypothesis and do interation.

From our Users Interview

Key Takeaway

1

Many AWOs are run by volunteers who hold day jobs

2

Some AWOs users are always on the go

3

AWOs users have various IT competency levels

All these point to the imminent gap that the pet ecosystem is growing. Both pets and pet owners need a systematic way of being managed between stakeholders ( both internal and external).

To further understand the ecocosystem

The team adopted the method of crafting 5 service blueprints follow by competitive analysis and drawing up a matrix of existing tools used by AWOs.

This helps us to dive deeper in identifying any pain points, gaps and opportunities present.

This also assist to support, validate and refute our assumptions

Service Blueprints

Service blueprints is a much clearer visual way to understand the process of AWOs and also help us to identify the gaps & opportunities which helps us empathise with our users in the entire journey of the services.

We have chosen service blueprint to understand the services and workflow of each AWOs of various size. We have also included the key stakeholder involved so to help us understand our AWOs better at each services.

Following are the things to consider when we came up with service blueprint:



KEY
SERVICES

• Animal Rescue
• Fostering
• Adoption
• Education

KEY STAKEHOLDERS

• Internal Staff
• External Partners
• AVA (Govt)

TYPES OF AWOs

• Large Welfare Organisation
• Mid Size Welfare Organisation
• Smaller Shelters

CURRENT WORKFLOW

• Paper Documentation
• Softwares like Microsoft office and Google drive.

From Service Blueprints

Key Takeaway

1

Primitive Paper documentation

2

Cumbersome & Unorganised

3

Documents are not easily traceable

4

Gaps in communication between staff/volunteers

5

Overall the process across AWOs lack structure

After synthesising our service blueprints, we uncovered gaps and opportunities in the way AWOs function.

Matrix of Existing Tools

We have drawn up a matrix to evaluate the effectiveness of these tools based on ease of use and extent of customisation.

Our product aims to be at the top right and it will be:

• Modular
• Scalable
• Flexible

Currently these are the existing tools in the market adopted by the ecosystem in running operations and services.
