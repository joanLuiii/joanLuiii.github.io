NUS Business School

Rebuilding and Redesigning of the Information Architecture
of National University Of Singapore, Business School

Process

Content audit, User interviews, Online Survey, Competitive analysis, Card sorting, Tree testing and Usability testing

Tools

Figma, Post-its, Papers, Optimal Workshop, Excel Sheets

Role

UX/UI Designer

The Challenge

In a group of 2, redesign the Information Architecture (IA) and key pages layout/wireframes of NUS Business School with the time duration of 2 weeks.

Design Process

1. Research

Research the current problems in the NUS Business School.
Understand who our target audience are.
Identify the frustrations and
of users.

2. Empathize

Put ourselves in the NUS Business School Student's shoes to understand what are they doing, feeling and thinking.

3. Define

Define the primary problems / pain point the NUS Business School is experiencing and what needs to be improved.

4. Ideate

Brainstorm creative solutions that will solve the student's pain points.

5. Prototype

Convert our ideation into design and build a clickable prototype using Figma.

6. Test

Do usability testing with NUS business school student to gather feedback, validate our hypothesis and do interation.

Research

Content Inventory Audit:

We were all in a state of shock when we see the amount of content and how deep the level of links it actually took us to.

This process also allowed us to understand the various programmes the school has offered and we have also learned the frustration of our users when they are looking for information.

Findings:

We found lots of duplicated links and information.

There were also a lot of links that brought us to different microsite.

Personas

Analyse our 3 persona

User Interview

We have decided to pick current students to narrow our research. The top 3 pain points that the current students are facing:

1) Find out if modules would meet degree requirements
2) Scheduling conflict of modules
3) Finding Career support

In order to further understands the root causes of these pain points, we interviewed a current Year 2 NUS Business Student.

From the Users Interview

Key Takeaway

1

NUS Business School website
Core modules he have to take.

2

External system (IVLE)
Modules that he have taken.

3

Manually comparison of modules
Modules that are left untaken.

4

“nusmods.com”
Modules information & to plan conflict in schedules.

5

Centralised Online Registration System (CORS)
Bid for modules

From here we understood that, the steps undertaken before registering modules were too tedious and complex, requiring one to plow through a multitude of websites and systems just to gather the information he required.
